# Fractureline Story Roadmap

This roadmap expands the Dystopia canon into future playable chapters.

## Chapter Length Rules

Chapter 1 is intentionally a taste of the world.

- Purpose: introduce Xav, Yve, Zelda, Ayker, Cybol, Diderram, Brinkton, the broken com, and the family notebook.
- Target first-play length: roughly 8 to 12 minutes.
- Design shape: quick orientation, first contact, first impossible family reveal, one meaningful ending.

Chapter 2 and onward should be meaningfully longer.

- Target first-play length: roughly 20 to 30 minutes per route.
- Each route should have more scenes than Chapter 1's average route.
- Each route should deepen the world rather than only extend the immediate plot.
- Each route should carry consequences from the Chapter 1 ending and preserve relevant variables/flags.

## Chapter 1 Current Endings

### Signal Path

Xav, Yve, and Zelda make careful contact. The bridge between eras is stable enough to study.

Future direction:

- Yve formalizes rules for contacting Zelda.
- Zelda maps changed parts of old Brinkton.
- The first serious Diderram contradiction appears.
- Xav and Yve try to verify Zelda's claims using Cybol records.

### Family Path

The family notebook proves that Zelda and Xav are connected. The Reivax-Adlez family line becomes central.

Future direction:

- Ari becomes important to the notebook and may be tied to firstborn inheritance rules.
- Zelda finds altered family records in the future.
- Xav must decide how much to involve his family.
- The family record may be a tool, a trap, or both.

### History Path

Ari touches the notebook and Zelda begins remembering multiple futures.

Future direction:

- Zelda carries conflicting memories.
- The future may partially rewrite itself into a cleaner but more dangerous Cybol restoration timeline.
- Xav, Yve, and Ari must figure out which change caused the split.
- The announcement about Cybol and Diderram becomes the next key event.

## Chapter 2 Route Targets

Chapter 2 should be much larger than Chapter 1.

Each Chapter 2 pack should include:

- At least 18 to 24 scenes over time, even if any single route only sees a subset.
- At least 8 to 12 meaningful player choices per route.
- At least one past-side Xav/Yve investigation branch.
- At least one future-side Zelda exploration branch.
- One route-specific complication that changes what Chapter 3 can start from.
- A clear ending that tees up the next major story event.

### Chapter 2: Signal Path

Working title: The Stable Signal.

Core expansion:

- Yve creates a contact protocol.
- Zelda discovers the peace archive under old Brinkton.
- The official Cybol/Diderram history begins to visibly contradict itself.
- Xav and Yve must decide whether to preserve the stable signal or risk a stronger intervention.

Possible beats:

1. Xav and Yve hide the com from university monitors.
2. Zelda maps new writing in the ruins.
3. The family notebook answers before Zelda does.
4. Yve creates rules for cross-time contact.
5. Zelda finds a sealed peace archive.
6. A Diderram recording contradicts Cybol's official exam history.
7. Xav and Yve discover tomorrow's Cybol announcement.
8. Chapter ends with a warning not to let Cybol make the announcement uncontested.

### Chapter 2: Family Path

Working title: The Firstborn Record.

Core expansion:

- The notebook becomes an active character-like object.
- Ari's role expands.
- Zelda finds evidence that the family line has been edited.
- The story explores whether the Reivax-Adlez line preserved the truth or helped hide it.

Possible beats:

1. Xav reads firstborn names from the notebook.
2. Zelda sees names change in the future.
3. Ari's name appears where Xav's should be.
4. Yve identifies the notebook as a ledger of choices and costs.
5. Zelda searches a sealed lineage archive.
6. Ari dreams of Zelda before Zelda sends the message.
7. The future asks for Ari's voiceprint.
8. Chapter ends with a message signed by Ari from an unknown future point.

### Chapter 2: History Path

Working title: The Second Future.

Core expansion:

- Zelda carries two future timelines at once.
- The new future is not simply better or worse. It is cleaner, more stable, and more dangerous because it absorbs rebellion into official history.
- Ari's notebook contact creates a new line of causality.
- Xav and Yve discover the next fixed event: a Cybol announcement about Diderram.

Possible beats:

1. Zelda wakes with two childhoods.
2. Marek briefly remembers both versions of himself.
3. The tunnels become partly ruins and partly restored civic corridors.
4. Xav asks Ari what the notebook showed her.
5. Yve builds a stabilizer to keep Zelda from being rewritten.
6. Zelda finds evidence that Cybol can survive collapse by renaming it restoration.
7. A younger recorded Zelda warns her older self.
8. Chapter ends with the instruction: Attend.

## Chapter 3 Direction

Working title: The Announcement.

Shared anchor event:

- Xav, Yve, and possibly Ari attend or infiltrate a Cybol announcement about Diderram.
- Zelda explores how that same announcement is remembered across each future route.
- The announcement should reveal that Cybol's public peace narrative is built from a hidden Diderram crisis.

Route-specific starts:

- Signal Path: Xav and Yve arrive prepared with a contact protocol and Zelda's archive evidence.
- Family Path: Ari and the notebook complicate who can safely intervene.
- History Path: Zelda's dual memories reveal two versions of the announcement before it happens.

Possible major choices:

- Let the announcement happen and record the lie.
- Interrupt the announcement publicly.
- Feed Yve's evidence to a trusted professor.
- Have Zelda alter or preserve future records.
- Protect Ari from the notebook or involve her deliberately.

## Chapter 4 Direction

Working title: Diderram's Silence.

Core goal:

- Move beyond Brinkton and introduce Diderram as more than a historical object.
- Show living Diderram voices in Xav's time and changed/erased Diderram memory in Zelda's time.

Possible beats:

- Xav and Yve meet a Diderram exchange student or activist.
- Zelda finds Diderram survivor records in the future.
- Cybol's utopia is revealed to depend on what it removed from Diderram.
- The player must decide whether to expose truth in a way that risks immediate harm.

## Chapter 5 Direction

Working title: The Cost of Utopia.

Core goal:

- Reveal the mechanism by which Cybol preserved its utopia.
- Tie the family notebook to that mechanism.
- Make Zelda's continued existence feel uncertain if Xav succeeds too much.

Possible beats:

- The notebook was built to preserve a line of witnesses.
- The firstborn rule may be a safety mechanism, not just tradition.
- Zelda's memories become increasingly unstable.
- Xav must choose between making a clean better future and preserving the person asking him to make it.

## Long-Term Mystery Threads

- Why does the notebook follow firstborn rules?
- Who created the first version of the notebook?
- Did Cybol fall, or did it transform into the future dystopia under another name?
- What exactly happened to Diderram?
- Is Zelda's dystopian leader connected to Xav's family line?
- Can Zelda survive a future where her own suffering never happened?
- Is Yve merely helping Xav, or does she become the intellectual architect of the future resistance?
- What role does Ari play in choosing which family line survives history?

## Implementation Notes

- Keep playable content in `.ink` chapter packs.
- Keep metadata, planning, and canon in docs.
- Do not put old temporary story content back into `apps/web/content`.
- Do not show implementation details like runtime/library names in player-facing UI.
- The homepage should contain setup and world briefing.
- The play page should start directly with the current scene.
