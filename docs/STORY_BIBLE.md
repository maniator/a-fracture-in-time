# Story Bible

## High Concept
A future society appears peaceful, efficient, and enlightened. In truth, its harmony is maintained through magical memory editing and engineered historical revisions. Two people on opposite sides of that system begin to tear reality apart.

## Setting
The future city-state is known as Lattice. It is clean, orderly, beautiful, and emotionally muted. Citizens live with reduced conflict because dangerous memories, impulses, and narratives are quietly altered.

Time manipulation is not mechanical. It is achieved through ritualized magic tied to memory, lineage, and historical anchors.

## Truth of the World
The utopia is real in its outcomes but false in its foundations. Violence, grief, dissent, and whole people have been erased from cultural memory to sustain social order.

## Factions

### The Keepers
Custodians of stability. They believe memory edits are merciful and necessary.

### The Fringe
Survivors, defectors, and temporal dissidents. They believe truth matters even if truth destabilizes everything.

## Main Characters

### Protector
- Born inside the system
- Disciplined, observant, loyal
- Genuinely wants to protect people
- Arc: loyalty -> doubt -> self-authored morality

### Dissenter
- Raised at the edge of collapsed futures
- Angry, resourceful, sacrificial
- Willing to break the world to restore truth
- Arc: vengeance -> clarity -> responsibility

## Themes
- Freedom vs safety
- Truth vs comfort
- Mercy vs control
- Who owns the past
- Whether peace built on lies is peace at all

## Tone
Quiet dread, emotional intimacy, fractured mystery, moral ambiguity.

## Chapter Spine

### Chapter 1: The Hairline Crack
Introduce both protagonists and the first impossible contradiction.

### Chapter 2: The Gentle Lie
Reveal how memory edits are normalized.

### Chapter 3: The Missing Funeral
Both characters discover evidence that an erased death still matters.

### Chapter 4: A City That Remembers Nothing
The player learns whole districts have vanished from collective history.

### Chapter 5: The First Undoing
A major player action visibly rewrites a past event.

### Chapter 6: The Cost of Mercy
Protector sees why the system was built. Dissenter sees what its removal would unleash.

### Chapter 7: The Children of the Broken Thread
The hidden survivors of edited timelines emerge.

### Chapter 8: The Hollow Archive
The core mechanism of revision is exposed.

### Chapter 9: Two Futures, One Wound
The protagonists become aware of one another more directly.

### Chapter 10: Fractureline
Final convergence. Choose what reality owes the living.

## Endings

### Perfect Silence
Order survives. Pain is minimized. Truth is buried.

### Open Ruin
Truth is restored. Society destabilizes. People become free and afraid.

### Wounded Memory
A compromise. Some truth returns, some scaffolding remains, and both protagonists are changed by the cost.
