# Story Bible

## High Concept

Fractureline is a dual-timeline narrative game set on the planet Ayker. One protagonist lives in a near-utopian past run by a civic power called Cybol. The other lives in the dystopian ruins of that same world, decades after Cybol's fall. They have found a way to speak to each other across time, and the choices they make together are rewriting both eras.

## Setting

### Ayker

A small, lush planet. Not Earth. The world has its own culture, political history, and technology. Home to two dominant powers, Cybol and Diderram, whose shared history and unresolved relationship is a central mystery of the game.

### Brinkton

The major city and civic hub of Cybol society. In Xav's time (874cy), it is a prosperous, polished city with a university, transit lines, and a carefully managed public history. In Zelda's time (23ac), it is ruins. Tunnels run beneath the old civic buildings, used by survivors as shelter and archive.

### Cybol

The dominant civic power in Xav's era. It presents itself as a force of peace, stability, and enlightened governance. Whether that peace is genuine or built on suppression is the central question of the early game.

### Diderram

The second major power on Ayker. Official Cybol history says Diderram accepted peace. Evidence in the ruins and in the family records suggests something darker. The relationship between Cybol and Diderram is one of the core political mysteries players uncover through Chapters 1–5.

## Truth of the World

The utopia appears real in its outcomes but may be false in its foundations. Communities, histories, and political alternatives may have been suppressed or erased to sustain Cybol's civic order. Zelda, living after the fall, is trying to understand what actually happened — and whether reaching back to the past makes things better or makes her a catastrophe.

## No Magic Rule

The story uses advanced science, time communication, strange devices, records, family artifacts, history manipulation, and sci-fi causality. There is no overt magic. What appears impossible — the notebook writing back, the com speaking across time — has technological and historical explanations that surface gradually through the narrative.

## Main Characters

### Xav Reivax

- Student at the University of Brinkton, 874cy
- Lives in Cybol society; experiences it as mostly functional and good
- Optimistic, a little scattered, genuinely loyal to his family
- His family's firstborn notebook and his frequently broken com become the first bridge to the future
- Arc: ordinary life → impossible contact → choosing how much historical truth to act on

### Yve Ettevy

- Xav's classmate and closest collaborator
- Sharper, more skeptical, and more methodical than Xav
- Her role is to verify claims, create protocols, and translate official language into plain meaning
- She notices problems in the official history before Xav does, but needs evidence before she acts
- Arc: productive skepticism → genuine belief → building systems that can survive contact with the truth

### Zelda Adlez

- Survivor in the ruins of old Brinkton, 23ac
- Descended from Xav's family line — likely his grandchild
- Finds a device sealed under a lecture hall that can contact Xav in the past
- Seeks to understand why Cybol fell and whether any change is possible across the timeline
- Carries the risk that changing the past will alter or erase her own memories
- Arc: desperate survival → careful strategy → choosing what kind of future is worth creating

### Ari Reivax

- Xav's younger sister
- Curious, observant, and apparently connected to the family notebook in ways that exceed Xav's understanding
- Her contact with the notebook in the History Path triggers a timeline split that Zelda can feel in real time
- Not a protagonist but a pivot: her choices carry consequences as large as Xav's

## Themes

- Whether peace built on managed history is peace at all
- What a person owes their family versus what they owe the truth
- Whether changing the past is an act of rescue or an act of violence against those who survived the original future
- Who gets to remember — and who gets to decide what is remembered
- The cost of silence when silence is the comfortable option

## Tone

Quiet dread, emotional intimacy, fractured mystery, moral ambiguity. The game is not action-driven. It is about what people choose to notice, what they choose to ignore, and what those choices cost.

## Chapter Spine

### Chapter 1: A Fracture in the Record

Xav and Yve are at the University of Brinkton preparing for a Cybol history exam. Xav's broken com receives a voice from the future: Zelda Adlez, calling from the ruins of old Brinkton. The family notebook begins to respond to the contact. The chapter ends in one of three routes depending on how Xav handles the notebook and his sister Ari.

**Endings:**
- Signal Path — cautious contact; the bridge between eras is stable
- Family Path — the family record is opened; the Reivax-Adlez lineage becomes central
- History Path — Ari touches the notebook; Zelda begins remembering two childhoods

### Chapter 2: Route Branches (three packs)

Each Chapter 1 ending unlocks a different Chapter 2:
- The Stable Signal (signal-path) — Yve builds a contact protocol; the Diderram contradiction begins to surface
- The Firstborn Record (family-path) — the family tree reveals missing names; Ari becomes a pivot
- The Second Future (history-path) — Zelda carries conflicting memories; the timeline is splitting

### Chapter 3: Governance and Consequence (three packs)

Chapter 3 continues each route and raises the question of what a functional future looks like if the past changes:
- The Relay Accord (signal-path)
- The Witness Ledger (family-path)
- The Public Memory Trial (history-path)

### Chapters 4–5: Consequence Branches and Merge Hubs (six and three packs)

Chapter 3 outcomes determine which of six Chapter 4 routes become available. Chapter 5 begins recombining routes into merge hubs. The braided architecture prevents exponential branching: major anchors converge players before the final act.

## Planned Endings

### Signal Stabilized

The bridge between eras becomes a public record. Truth is preserved at the cost of short-term stability.

### Family First

The Reivax-Adlez lineage survives across the timeline change. Personal history is protected; civic history is only partially revised.

### The Second Past

The timeline settles into a version Zelda partially shaped. What she remembers of the original future begins to fade.
